# Open Source & Science with Foundation Models — Percy Liang (Stanford)

**Course:** Large Language Model Agents
**Video:** [YouTube](https://youtube.com/watch?v=f3KKx9LWntQ)

## TL;DR
Percy Liang argues that access shapes research — the type of AI access available (API, open weights, open source) fundamentally determines what research is possible. He presents three levels of access with increasing research capability, showcases generative agents that simulate human social behavior, and demonstrates that 2-hour interviews can create AI agents replicating individual human behavior with high fidelity (85%+) across social science experiments.

## Key Takeaways
1. **Access shapes research:** Historical examples (internet text → computational NLP, crowdsourcing → annotated datasets, GPUs → deep learning) show that available resources determine entire research paradigms.
2. **Three levels of AI access:** API access (behavioral measurement only), open weights (fine-tuning, distillation), open source (full reproducibility, architectural innovation) — each enables progressively deeper research.
3. **Generative agents simulate social worlds:** LLM-powered agents in virtual environments exhibit emergent social behaviors including information diffusion, party planning, and social relationship dynamics.
4. **Interview-based agent creation:** 2-hour structured interviews capture enough of an individual's personality, experiences, and perspectives to create AI agents that replicate their behavior with 85%+ fidelity across economic games and social science experiments.
5. **Open source is essential for fundamental progress:** Without access to data, architecture, and training algorithms, research is stuck in local optima defined by API boundaries.

## Detailed Notes
The lecture covers the historical relationship between access and research breakthroughs, the three-tier access framework with examples of what each enables, the generative agents paper showing emergent social behavior in simulated environments, the scaling of agent simulation through interview-based creation with 1000 demographically diverse participants, HELM benchmark for transparent foundation model evaluation, and the argument for open-source AI as necessary for scientific understanding.

## Notable Quotes
1. "Access shapes research — most of the time we have our heads down working on whatever problem, but historically, access has dramatically changed the type of work that has happened."
2. "In order to make fundamental progress we need to question the data, the model architecture, the training algorithm."
3. "The true promise of this technology is can we simulate actual real people."
