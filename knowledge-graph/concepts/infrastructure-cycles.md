# Infrastructure Cycles

The historical pattern where new general-purpose technologies create infrastructure explosions followed by hoarding, panics, crashes, and eventual stabilization through standards and institutions. Observed in steel (1867-1895), fiber optics (2000s), DRAM, uranium, and now compute. Digital cycles average ~2.8 years; physical ~6.3 years.
